§ Programmes

Five stages. One unbroken journey, age three to fifteen

From the very first steps of Playgroup through the threshold of senior secondary, every level at The Paradox Academy is designed with rigour, creativity, and pastoral care — preparing scholars of character for the world that awaits them.

FrameworkCBC · 2-6-3-3
StagesPG through Grade 9
Ages2 to 15 years
ComingGCSE pathway
§ 01 Playgroup (PG) · Ages 2–4

Praeludium — Playgroup.

Our Playgroup programme is the very first chapter of a child's educational journey at The Paradox Academy. Designed for children aged three to four, it is a gentle, joyful introduction to structured learning — where play is the primary language and every activity is purposeful.

Qualified early childhood educators guide each child through sensory exploration, creative expression, and guided discovery in small, intimate groups. Our youngest learners leave Playgroup confident, curious, and perfectly prepared to transition into Pre-Primary.

Key focus areas
  • Sensory exploration & fine motor skills01
  • Language development — listening & speaking02
  • Number & shape awareness03
  • Socialisation & turn-taking04
  • Music, movement & rhythm05
  • Self-care & independence routines06
§ 02 PP1 & PP2 · Ages 4–6

Initium — Early Years.

Our Early Years programme provides a warm, nurturing foundation for lifelong learning. Through play-based, inquiry-led pedagogy, we guide our youngest scholars in developing the language, numeracy, motor, and social competencies they need to flourish.

Mornings are anchored by routine and song; afternoons by exploration and rest. Every space is designed for small hands and bright eyes — and every educator understands that, at this age, character is being shaped just as surely as skill.

Key focus areas
  • Language & literacy · EN / SW01
  • Early numeracy & mathematical reasoning02
  • Creative arts, music & movement03
  • Social-emotional learning & values04
  • Environmental exploration05
  • Psychomotor & fine motor development06
§ 03 Grade 1 – 3 · Ages 6–9

Fundamentum — Lower Primary.

In Lower Primary, learners transition from play-centred discovery into structured academic engagement. Our educators employ a blend of activity-based learning and guided instruction to establish strong foundations in literacy, numeracy, science, and social competence.

It is here that handwriting becomes legible, that times tables become second nature, and that the first independent sentences appear in writing books that children carry home with pride.

CBC learning areas
  • English Language01
  • Kiswahili / Kenyan Sign Language02
  • Mathematics03
  • Environmental Activities04
  • Hygiene & Nutrition05
  • Creative Arts · Art, Craft, Music06
  • Physical & Health Education07
  • Religious Education · CRE / IRE / HRE08
§ 04 Grade 4 – 6 · Ages 9–12

Progressio — Upper Primary.

Upper Primary deepens academic rigour and introduces learners to specialised subject disciplines. At this level, we cultivate critical thinking, independent research, collaborative problem-solving, and the exploration of individual talents.

Pupils are carefully prepared for the transition to Junior Secondary School, including the national Kenya Primary School Education Assessment (KPSEA) at the end of Grade 6.

CBC learning areas
  • English & Kiswahili01
  • Mathematics02
  • Integrated Science03
  • Social Studies · History, Geography, Citizenship04
  • Agriculture & Nutrition05
  • Creative Arts & Sports06
  • Pre-Technical & Pre-Career Education07
  • Foreign language enrichment · optional08
§ 05 Grade 7 – 9 · Ages 12–15

Ascensio — Junior Secondary.

Junior Secondary School represents a pivotal stage in every learner's formation. We deliver the JSS curriculum with academic depth, practical engagement, and careful guidance as our scholars prepare for Senior Secondary and beyond. Learners study a combination of core subjects and elective pathways that allow them to explore their strengths and interests, supported throughout by mentorship and a dedicated careers programme.

Core subjects
  • English Language01
  • Kiswahili Language02
  • Mathematics03
  • Integrated Science04
  • Health Education05
  • Pre-Technical Studies06
  • Social Studies07
  • Religious Education08
  • Business Studies09
  • Agriculture10
  • Life Skills Education11
Elective pathways
  • Visual ArtsA
  • Performing Arts · Music, Drama, FilmB
  • Home ScienceC
  • Computer Science & TechnologyD
  • Foreign Languages · FR / DE / ZH / ARE
  • Kenyan Sign LanguageF
  • Indigenous LanguagesG

Pupils choose electives based on interest and aptitude. Assessment combines formative work, school projects, and the national KPSEA — informing pathway selection for Senior Secondary.

§ 06 Beyond the classroom

Where education extends far beyond the timetable.

Our co-curricular programme is integral to a Paradox formation. Sports, arts, clubs, service — these develop confidence, teamwork, leadership, and lifelong skills the timetable alone never could.

A

Sports & athletics

Football, netball, volleyball, handball, athletics. Inter-school competitions, an annual Sports Day, cross-country, fitness education.

B

Creative & performing arts

Choir, instrumental programmes, drama, theatre, public speaking, painting, sculpture, craft. Annual talent shows and cultural celebrations.

C

Clubs & societies

Science & Innovation, Debate, Eco-Warriors, Maths Challenge, Readers' Guild, Young Leaders' Council, Journalism & Media.

D

Digital literacy

Computer literacy from Lower Primary. Introduction to coding and computational thinking. Digital citizenship and online safety.

E

Service & values

Community outreach and service-learning projects. Mentorship and peer support. Character education and values assemblies.

F

Pastoral care

Restorative discipline. Class teachers who know every child. Guidance counsellors at JSS. Open-door access for families.
