§ Student Life

A day at Paradox. Vibrant, purposeful, enriching

The experiences beyond the timetable are as formative as those within it. Our learners grow through friendship, teamwork, creativity, leadership, and service — becoming champions in every dimension of their lives.

§ 01 A typical day

From assembly to the final bell.

Our school day is carefully structured to balance focused academic learning with movement, creativity, and social interaction. Every moment is intentional.

7:30 AM

Arrival & settle-in

Pupils arrive, greet their class teacher, and prepare for the day ahead. Early arrivals enjoy quiet reading time in the classroom or library.

8:00 AM

Morning assembly

A cherished tradition of shared purpose. National anthem, school prayer, announcements, and a moment of reflection that orients the whole community.

8:30 AM — 12:30 PM

Core lessons

The intellectual heart of the day. Focused academic sessions in literacy, numeracy, science, and the humanities, with short breaks woven in to keep minds fresh.

10:30 AM

Mid-morning break

Snack, fresh air, and friendship. Pupils stretch their legs and reconnect with classmates before the next block of learning.

12:30 PM

Lunch & recess

A balanced, nutritious meal in our dining hall, followed by play, conversation, and movement on the grounds.

1:30 PM — 3:30 PM

Afternoon learning

Practical sessions, creative arts, and inquiry projects. JSS learners take subject-specialist lessons; younger pupils explore through hands-on activity.

3:30 PM — 4:30 PM

Clubs & activities

Sports, music, drama, debate, eco-club, coding. Co-curricular life is integral — not an extra.

4:30 PM

Dismissal & guided study

JSS learners may stay for guided self-study with mentor support; younger pupils are released to families. The day closes with the same care it began.

§ 02 Pastoral care & wellbeing

Every child known, every child cared for.

Every child at Paradox Academy has a class teacher who knows them well and monitors their academic progress, emotional wellbeing, and social development. We maintain an open-door policy for parents and provide regular updates through reports, parent conferences, and direct communication.

Our approach to discipline is restorative — focused on building self-regulation, empathy, and personal responsibility rather than punishment. For Junior Secondary learners, dedicated mentors and guidance counsellors provide additional support during this pivotal stage.

What you can expect

A class teacher who knows your childTheir progress, their temperament, their friendships
Restorative disciplineEmpathy and responsibility, not punishment
Open-door policyDirect access to staff and leadership
Regular communicationReports, conferences, real-time updates
§ 03 Events & traditions

The rhythm of the school year.

01

Opening Day & welcome

The whole community gathers to mark the start of each term — a tradition of belonging and shared purpose.

02

Sports Day

Athletics championships, inter-house competition, family picnics. The annual celebration of physical excellence.

03

Cultural Day

Talent showcases, dance, drama, music, and the rich cultural heritage of our community on full display.

04

Science fair

Innovation exhibitions where pupils present projects, experiments, and inventions to families and judges.

05

Academic competitions

Interclass debate, mathematics challenges, spelling bees, and inter-school tournaments throughout the year.

06

Prize-Giving Day

The annual celebration of academic, creative, sporting, and character achievements. The signature event of our calendar.
